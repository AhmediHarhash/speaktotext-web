# SpeakToText Story Bible

## North Star

SpeakToText is the voice-first writing system that turns raw thought into the right output everywhere.

The site should feel like one connected product world: dark premium space, human gold voice energy, cold silver intelligence, and real text proof. Every section must either advance the story or help the user trust the product.

Primary headline direction:

> Speak once. Get the right output everywhere.

Primary subheadline direction:

> SpeakToText turns voice into polished writing, prompts, replies, summaries, and business-ready drafts across your daily apps.

## Motion Rules

- GSAP owns scroll storytelling, pinned scenes, horizontal travel, text reveals, and section timing.
- R3F and Three.js are used only for true 3D worlds: the hero journey, the Anywhere wall, and future spatial product scenes.
- Text stays real DOM text for SEO, clarity, accessibility, and honest product proof.
- No fake cinematic UI. If a video, 3D model, or poster does not exist yet, the site uses the current honest product fallback.
- Premium means controlled: fewer effects, stronger timing, cleaner typography, and no random glows.

## Story Frames

1. **Dark Entry**
   - Visitor enters a quiet black/gold world.
   - Hero text appears first, with voice energy implied in the environment.
   - Asset need: future desktop and mobile hero journey video or WebGL scene.

2. **Voice Appears**
   - A gold waveform, core, or cursor path becomes the visual language for the whole page.
   - It should feel like thought becoming a signal, not generic AI decoration.
   - Asset need: voice core, waveform ribbon, or Blender/WebGL camera path.

3. **Writing Anywhere**
   - The voice signal reaches the app universe.
   - Current Anywhere 3D wall stays because it proves breadth and creates the first spatial moment.
   - Asset need: no replacement yet; later optimize the logo wall as one GPU-aware system.

4. **Raw Thought**
   - Demos section begins with huge transparent spoken text across the viewport.
   - The visitor sees that the product starts with imperfect human language.
   - Asset need: none; use real DOM text from `DEMO_TABS`.

5. **Polished Work**
   - The rough language resolves into smaller app proof panels.
   - Each app proves a different business use: prompt, brief, email, post, notes.
   - Asset need: final product examples, not screenshots unless they are real and clean.

6. **Simple Mechanic**
   - How It Works explains the action after the emotional proof: hold, speak, release.
   - The current visual plate remains the main asset until a mobile 9:16 version exists.
   - Asset need: optional mobile vertical plate/poster.

7. **Feature Expansion**
   - Feature cards show SpeakToText is not a one-action trick.
   - The story expands into unlimited transcription, clean writing, formats, history, and privacy.
   - Asset need: none for now; keep cards controlled and readable.

8. **Business Decision**
   - Pricing and FAQ calm the world down.
   - The visitor should now understand what it does, why it matters, and how to start.
   - Asset need: none.

9. **Final Resolve**
   - CTA brings the gold voice energy back into one calm action.
   - The world should feel finished, not louder.
   - Asset need: future final CTA still or subtle 3D object.

## Feature Pillars

- **Voice Dictation Anywhere:** speak naturally and turn messy speech into clean writing across apps, browsers, editors, email, and AI tools.
- **App-Aware Output:** shape output for the destination: email, prompt, plain text, coding agent, browser field, or document.
- **Prompt Builder:** turn spoken ideas into structured prompts for coding, design, planning, image/video generation, reviews, and AI workflows.
- **Assistant Commands:** use commands like make shorter, make professional, turn into bullets, summarize this, or draft a reply.
- **Selected Text Actions:** highlight text, speak the action, and get a rewrite, summary, reply, or prompt from that context.
- **Smart Email Drafting:** draft replies, quote responses, follow-ups, support messages, scheduling replies, and business emails with the right tone.
- **Style Memory:** learn tone, reply length, greetings, signoffs, and preferred phrasing so outputs feel more like the user.
- **Knowledge Packs:** let businesses add products, prices, plans, FAQs, policies, and company info for approved replies.
- **Draft-Only Business Playbooks:** reusable flows for quotes, invoice reminders, support replies, product answers, and policy responses.
- **Plan-Based Credits:** Premium, Ultimate, and Business include monthly credits for assistant drafting and advanced workflows.

## Section Roadmap

- **Current Core:** Hero, Anywhere, Demos, How It Works, Feature Story, Pricing, FAQ, CTA.
- **Next Recommended Section:** App-Aware Output, placed after Anywhere or merged into the Demos transition, because it explains why SpeakToText is more than dictation.
- **Second Recommended Section:** Prompt Builder / Assistant Commands, as a kinetic command scene with huge transparent commands turning into finished outputs.
- **Business Expansion Section:** Knowledge Packs + Business Playbooks, later in the page before Pricing, once the product/package details are real.
- **Style Memory Section:** keep for a later trust/product depth section; it needs careful wording so it feels premium and private, not creepy.

## Hero Asset Brief

- Desktop: 16:9 or 21:9, 8-12 seconds, seamless loop, WebM and MP4.
- Mobile: 9:16 version, same concept, not cropped desktop.
- Poster: desktop and mobile stills from the same art direction.
- Mood: black, gold, silver, restrained cold highlights.
- Subject: voice energy traveling through a premium 3D writing world.
- Avoid: fake dashboards, fake app screenshots, purple AI styling, overexposed bloom, stock footage.

## Acceptance Criteria

- A user can describe the story after scrolling: "I speak rough thoughts, and it turns them into polished writing anywhere."
- Each section occupies its own screen moment without feeling disconnected from the next.
- The before/after proof is readable on desktop and mobile.
- The site can support future feature sections without changing the story spine.
- Reduced-motion users still receive the same story in a readable stacked layout.
